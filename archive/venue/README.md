# INTO THE BLUE

## Summary

INTO THE BLUE is a jazz live venue located in Machida, Tokyo, Japan.

Founded by Osamu Omata on 2 April 2014, the venue was established to provide a place where people could casually enjoy live jazz performances within the local community.

The venue hosts professional concerts, amateur sessions, student performances and community music activities.

---

# Historical Overview

Osamu Omata, originally from Kamimizo in Sagamihara, worked for approximately forty years in the sales division of a major transportation company.

As a lifelong jazz enthusiast, he frequently visited jazz clubs in Roppongi and Kannai before deciding to establish a venue closer to his local area.

The venue officially opened on 2 April 2014.

The venue supports student performances including:

- Showa University of Music
- Senzoku Gakuen College of Music

---

# Cultural Significance

The venue serves as a regional cultural hub connecting professional musicians, students, amateur performers and local audiences.

Its name derives from an original composition, "Into the Blue", written by American pianist Philip Strange to commemorate the venue's opening.
